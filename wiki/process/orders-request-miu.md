# Requesting Orders at MIU

Once you know your orders type
([../orders-types/choosing-orders-type.md](../orders-types/choosing-orders-type.md)),
this is the mechanical path. (MIU Orders-Type Matrix, 6 Mar 2026)

## Prerequisites — check these FIRST [C009]

Orders requests stall on currency. Before submitting, be current on:

- **Medical**
- **GTCC** (Government Travel Charge Card)
- **Primary Residence** (on file)
- **APACS** — additionally required for any OCONUS travel [C006]

## Lead times

Submit the **Orders Request Form in the Correspondence Tracker**:

| Trip | Submit by | Claim |
|---|---|---|
| Offsite IDT / AT, CONUS | 45 days prior | [C004] |
| ADOS, CONUS | NLT 2 weeks prior | [C005] |
| Offsite IDT / AT, OCONUS | 60 days prior | [C006] |
| ADOS, OCONUS | NLT 3 weeks prior | [C007] |

For HTC drills (no orders request needed):

- $750 travel voucher in DTS-R NLT 15 days prior [C002]
- Notify MIU_Operations@usmc.mil NLT 1 week prior [C016]
- Billeting Request Form with S-4 if lodging needed [C016]

## The clock that bites: commercial air

The orders lead time is NOT the whole timeline. If your trip involves
commercial air, the **T3/TOP submission ladder starts earlier than your orders
deadline** — up to D-65 for group charters and D-35 for CONUS commercial air.
An ADOS request 2 weeks out is fine for orders and far too late for a flight.
Check [t3-timelines.md](t3-timelines.md) the moment air travel is possible.

## If you're late

Missed windows aren't automatically fatal, but they escalate: see
[t3-waivers.md](t3-waivers.md) for the adjudication chain.
