# T3 Late Submissions and Waivers

Late doesn't mean impossible — it means escalation. The adjudication process
is ForO 3000-52.1, Ch 5, para 3.j (p. 5-6); verbatim text:
[../sources/foro-3000-52-1/ch5-t3-logistics.md](../sources/foro-3000-52-1/ch5-t3-logistics.md).

## Who must endorse [C028]

| How late | Endorsement required |
|---|---|
| Any timeline in the [D-ladder](t3-timelines.md) exceeded, up to D-16 | **MSC G-3/5** |
| Timeline exceeded within 15 days of execution | **MSC Chief of Staff (CoS)** |

Related tripwire [C034]: orders not approved by D-15 → reservations cancelled,
resubmission itself requires the CoS waiver (Ch 5, para 3.f(5), p. 5-5).

## What the endorsement must contain [C029]

An email endorsement attached to the TEEP request summary page, stating:

1. **Operational necessity** — why this travel must happen despite lateness
2. **Acceptance of all associated costs** — late booking is expensive
3. **Acceptance of risk** caused by lack of carrier availability — seats may
   simply not exist

## What this means in practice

- The waiver chain runs through your MSC (for MIU: Force Headquarters Group),
  not through MIU itself — start with your S-3/S-1 immediately.
- A CoS-level endorsement is a big ask. The unit is telling a general-officer
  staff that its planning failed and the government should pay a premium for
  it. Getting the request in on time
  ([t3-timelines.md](t3-timelines.md)) is always the cheaper path.
- Manifest additions inside the window are also adjudicated: new travelers on
  an existing movement require a new T3 request AND the adjudication process
  (Ch 5, para 3.h(4), p. 5-5).
