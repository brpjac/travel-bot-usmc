# Distance Thresholds

The mileage numbers that change your travel outcome. Distances are official
DTOD distances, not odometer readings.

| Threshold | What changes | Source |
|---|---|---|
| **50 miles** from HTC | Above it: Offsite IDT / AT / ADOS territory. Below it: standard IDT, no travel entitlements [C003] | (MIU Orders-Type Matrix, 6 Mar 2026) |
| **75 miles** from residence | One factor in the case-by-case higher-than-cap IDT reimbursement exception | (JTR, par. 032304.B.2, p. 3B-8) |
| **150 miles** one way | Minimum unit-to-residence distance for IDT Travel Reimbursement — up to $750/round trip [C041], billet + BIC eligibility gates apply [C042] | (JTR, par. 032304, pp. 3B-7 to 3B-8; MARADMIN 157/25, paras 3, 5) |
| **400 miles** one way / **800 miles** round trip | At or under: POV automatically advantageous, mileage paid, no comparison. Over: AO case-by-case, constructed-travel comparison [C018] | (JTR, par. 020210, Table 2-10, p. 2-23) |

## How they interact — worked examples

- **Drill 40 miles away**: standard IDT. No entitlements; the choice is CO
  lodging exception or ADOS if support is genuinely needed —
  [../orders-types/choosing-orders-type.md](../orders-types/choosing-orders-type.md).
- **Drill 200 miles away**: Offsite IDT (>50) [C003]; also crosses 150, so
  IDT-R (up to $750/round trip) may apply IF the billet and BIC gates pass
  [C041] [C042] — but note IDT-R and offsite-IDT travel funding can't be
  combined [C044]. POV mileage fine (<400 one-way) [C018].
- **AT 500 miles away**: >400 one-way, so flying is the default assumption;
  driving needs AO approval and a constructed-cost comparison [C018] — and a
  flight means the T3 clock:
  [../process/t3-timelines.md](../process/t3-timelines.md).
