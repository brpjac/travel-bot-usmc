# POV Mileage and Constructed Travel

Rules for using your own vehicle on travel-bearing orders. Source: JTR par.
020210 (ed. 07/01/2026); verbatim text:
[../sources/jtr/par-020210-pov-use.md](../sources/jtr/par-020210-pov-use.md).

## The distance rule [C018]

| Official distance (DTOD) | Result |
|---|---|
| **≤400 miles one way / ≤800 miles round trip** | POV is automatically considered advantageous to the Government. Mileage paid at the TDY rate, no cost comparison required. |
| **>400 one way / >800 round trip** | AO may authorize POV case-by-case if advantageous — expect a constructed travel comparison. |

(JTR, par. 020210, Table 2-10, p. 2-23)

## If you drive instead of the authorized mode

Reimbursement is limited to the **constructed cost** of the authorized
transportation: ticket price plus TMC fee, and you get the lesser of that or
your mileage. Parking and tolls incurred on the drive are NOT reimbursed in
that scenario. (JTR, par. 020210, Table 2-10 row 3 and para F, pp. 2-23 to 2-24.)

## Other facts worth knowing

- Nobody can order you to drive your POV, and a passenger in a POV gets per
  diem but no transportation reimbursement (JTR, par. 020210, p. 2-22).
- Mileage rates differ by vehicle type (auto, motorcycle, airplane) and change
  periodically — look up the current TDY mileage rate on the DTMO site rather
  than trusting a stale number.
- On standard IDT there is no mileage entitlement — the $750 voucher is the
  mechanism ([idt-travel-reimbursement.md](idt-travel-reimbursement.md)).
