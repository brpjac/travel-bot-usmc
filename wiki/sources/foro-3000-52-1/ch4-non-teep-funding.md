# ForO 3000-52.1, Chapter 4 — Non-TEEP Funding (verbatim)

## 1. Introduction (p. 4-1)

Funding executed within the T3 database that is not associated with TEEP
authorized events will be considered non-TEEP funded events. Tracking non-TEEP
funding within the T3 database ensures the Command is able to capture the
transportation costs of personnel, equipment and supplies not associated with
the TEEP. Non-TEEP funding and TEEP funding added together provides a holistic
picture of the cost to train the Selected Marine Corps Reserve. Funding for
non-TEEP transportation requirements can be funded using O&MMC via MARFORRES
OCO allocation, RPMC for schools funding and other personnel movements
articulated below or O&MMCR for movement of equipment and supplies.

## 2. Overseas Contingency Operations (OCO 1106 O&M) (p. 4-1)

a. The Office of the Secretary of Defense (OSD) designates which overseas
operations qualify for OCO funding. Operations that employ activated and
mobilized MARFORRES personnel in order to participate in military actions,
operations or hostilities against an enemy of the United States qualify for
OCO funding and activations are generally conducted under Section 12302, 10
U.S.C. authority.

b. OCO funding supports travel & per diem as well as purchases of supplies in
support of mobilization requirements.

c. Requirements for OCO funding will be identified on the "Mobilization
Funding" line of the Expense Categories page of the TEEP request.

## 3. Schools Funding (1108 RPMC) (p. 4-1)

a. Refresher and Proficiency Training (PID RPT) is the primary funding source
for formal schools with the exception of Reserve Professional Military
education (RPME). RPT funds pay and allowances, travel and per diem. RPT
funding should be identified as PID RPT on the MROWS orders.

b. Career Development Training (PID CDT) funds Professional Military Education
(PME) programs within MARFORRES. The RPME courses are institutional
requirements for promotion eligibility. CDT funds pay and allowances, travel
and per diem. CDT funding should be identified as PID CDT in the MROWS orders.

c. When submitting a TEEP request to support Formal Schools, identify
requirements on the "Schools Funding" line of the Expense Categories page of
the TEEP request.

## 4. Other Non-TEEP RPMC Funding (p. 4-2)

The following is a list of known non-TEEP events that are tracked through the
T3 database and cover pay and allowances, travel and per diem for reservists,
unless DTS is used for the travel authorization:

a. COMMARFORRES ordered Legal/Court Martial (witnesses or members) use PID CM
in MROWS.
b. Funeral Honors use PID G in MROWS.
c. Marksmanship use PID H in MROWS.
d. Service or Mission Support use PID F in MROWS.
e. Global War on Terrorism use PID GWT in MROWS.
f. Inspections use PID INS.
g. Boards and Conferences use PID J in MROWS.
h. Operational Training use PID Q in MROWS.

## 5. Operational & Maintenance Marine Corps Reserve (O&MMCR 1107) (p. 4-2)

a. O&MMCR funding covers the transportation of things (TOT) in support of
non-TEEP funded requirements. (1) The funding can be used for the
redistribution of equipment and supplies. (2) O&MMCR also funds maintenance of
equipment not associated with approved TEEP funded exercises. (3)
Additionally, it funds Third Party Delivery System (TDS) such as FEDEX, UPS
and DHL which is also known as the Small Package Program (SPP).

b. Types of shipments funded by O&MMCR include: (1) Cargo Movement Operations
System shipments (2) Non Cargo Movement Operations System shipments (3)
Transport Distribution Service shipments
