# ForO 3000-52.1, Chapter 5 — T3 Logistical Considerations (verbatim)

## 1. Introduction (p. 5-1)

To the logistician, the T3 is viewed as a planning and budgeting document that
identifies the key logistical considerations of TOT, TOP and sustainment
through Exercise Support and Post Exercise Maintenance. All of these
considerations are essential during the planning process in order to preserve
optimal training opportunities at minimal cost. Refer to reference (d) for
detailed instructions on policy and procedures for logistical issues.

## 2. Transportation of Things (TOT) (pp. 5-1 to 5-2)

a. Units are required to submit accurate dimensional data of length, width,
height, weight, NSN and nomenclature of items to be shipped. This information
will determine mode of shipment. A "Small Package" is defined as any shipment
under 150 pounds that does not exceed a girth (length + width + height) of 130
inches or a value of than $50,000. Any shipment outside these parameters will
be shipped as general cargo. For OCONUS (includes Alaska, Hawaii, and Puerto
Rico) locations, the weight should not exceed 300 lbs. and no single package
should exceed 119 inches in length and 165 inches in length and overall size
(L,W,H) combined.

b. TOT funds the movement of equipment and supplies to exercise location and
Ports of Embarkation (POE).

c. **Submission Deadlines for Receipt of TOT requests by DMO:**
(1) 60 days prior to shipment date for OCONUS.
(2) 30 days prior to shipment date for CONUS.
(3) 7 days prior to shipment date for Small Package Program (SPP).

d. Requests must be submitted into the T3 database as a TEEP funded or
Non-TEEP funded request. T3 request must be approved before movement will be
scheduled.

e. Each MSC is responsible for ensuring a signed DD1149 is either T3 generated
or attached to the TOT request. The DD1149 must have a valid Transportation
Account Code (TAC) assigned by the MSC G3 and the signature of the MSC TAC
approver who is appointed by the MSC Chief of Staff. Additionally, the MSC G3
is responsible for submitting to DMO a list of TAC approvers, which will be
used to match and for ensuring each DD1149 has been properly approved for the
commitment of funds. Any request missing this information will be cancelled by
DMO.

f. Weapons and sensitive material can only be shipped utilizing a Surface
Deployment and Distribution Command (SDDC) approved bonded carrier. For any
bonded cargo (i.e. weapons and controlled cryptographic items (CCI)), units
must provide federal national stock number, nomenclature, serial number,
controlled inventory item code (CIIC), and security risk category (SRC) for
each item. Only approved serialized bolt seals (NSN: 5340-01-260-9935,
5340-01-542-7347, 5340-01-542-7359) are utilized when transporting cargo
requiring Transportation Protective Services (TPS). Note: for additional
security, the shipper may employ a cable seal (NSN: 5340-00-084-1570) to the
top of the container. Personnel receiving weapons and sensitive material must
be pre-designated Responsible Officers, assigned in writing, and members of
the unit's advance party. Direct all questions to the MARFORRES G-4.

g. Units are required to submit receipt of signed Bill of Lading into T3
within 3 working days of delivery. Additionally, for bonded cargo, units are
required to submit a signed signature and tally record (DD form 1907), a
signed and completed motor vehicle inspection (transporting hazardous
materials) (DD form 626), and a copy of the driver's license (front and back).
Units must ensure that block 14 (satellite motor surveillance system) is
checked on the DD form 626. These additional documents must be submitted into
T3 within 24 hour after delivery. The MSCs are ultimately responsible for
ensuring this process is completed.

h. Units are reminded that all shipments that are packed, crated, and/or
containerized must contain a complete DD form 1750 (packing list) of all
contents. The serial number is required to be annotated on the DD1149 and/or
packing list for all serialized gear. All equipment must be fully accounted
for on the DD 1750 so that all contents can be declared to file any potential
Transportation Discrepancy Reports (TDR) for lost, pilfered, or damaged cargo
and equipment while in transit.

i. Units are reminded that TOT requests requiring commercial truck line-haul
must include a load plan. Ensure the retrograde cargo data takes into account
all consumables used during an exercise. Load plans will identify the desired
type of carrier equipment (e.g. 48' flatbed, lowboy with ramps) required to
execute shipment. All additional support requirements, if required, will be
identified on the TOT request (e.g. MHE, ramps, crane, and forklifts).

j. Close communication must be maintained between the unit, receiving DMO, and
MARFORRES DMO when dealing with bonded cargo. No shipment will be executed
unless there is confirmed, in writing (T3), verification that qualified or
authorized personnel (i.e. armorer, CCI custodian, etc.) will be present to
receipt for the shipment. Maximum effort must be made to utilize DMO
installations as receiving points. In situations where Home Training Centers
(HTCs) are not co-located with a servicing DMO, they must communicate to
MARFORRES DMO, in writing (T3), that unit assumes receiving responsibility for
the cargo prior to MARFORRES DMO approving the movement.

## 3. Transportation of Personnel (TOP) (pp. 5-3 to 5-6)

a. All SMCR travelers funded by TEEP/Non-TEEP dollars, and requiring DMO to
schedule travel, will need an approved request submitted in T3. This will
generate a TOP request for action by DMO. Units must use quality control
measures for the traveler's data submitted in TOP requests to avoid
reservations and ticketing delays. All TOP requests must have an attached copy
of authenticated MROWS orders prior to DMO purchasing a ticket.

b. Reserve Marines using DTS to schedule travel for a Non-TEEP funded event
are not required to submit a T3 request.

c. Active Duty/Active Reserve Marines using DTS that do not utilize TEEP
funding, are not required to submit a T3 request.

d. **Reserve Personnel Marine Corps (RPMC) Travel.**

(1) Individual Travel. Commanders of individual reserve travelers on orders
that are not eligible for DTS will schedule their travel by submitting a TEEP
request in T3 database. Commanders of individual travelers using TEEP funding
and executing orders that are DTS eligible, will also submit a TEEP request
for funding and schedule their travel in DTS. Non-TEEP funded travelers using
DTS will not submit a request in T3. Individuals are not authorized to make
travel arrangements outside the servicing Travel Management Company, such as
through local travel agencies, websites, or airports.

(2) Group Commercial Travel. Group movements of 2 to 99 passengers. All
travelers must be departing from the same location on the same date and time
and arriving at the same destination on the same date and time. Requires
submission of a TEEP request in T3 database.

(3) Commercial Charter. Group movements of 100 passengers or more within CONUS
are scheduled by DMO. Group movements of 100 passengers or more OCONUS are
scheduled by the Strategic Mobility Office (SMO) IAW paragraph 4. The below
planning factors are pertinent to CONUS commercial charter movements only:
(a) Departure Date. (b) Passenger count. (c) Aerial Port of Embarkation (APOE)
and Aerial Port of Debarkation (APOD). (d) Requires submission of TEEP request
in T3 database.

(4) Commercial Bus Travel. Commercial bus used for transportation to and from
airport or exercise/event location. Buses to and from airport will be
scheduled at the same time as the air movement. Not to be used for in and
around training area. Requires submission of a TEEP request in T3. Units are
required to complete the DD Form 1341 and upload it to T3. This notifies DMO
of the execution of the movement and that satisfactory services were provided.
DMO will then proceed with bus payments. Units have 24 hours to submit this
form. Failure to submit this form will delay the payment for services and will
require justification from the MSC CoS.

(5) Commercial Rental Vehicle. Commercial rental vehicle used to and from
exercise/event location requires submission of a TEEP request in T3.

e. Temporary Additional Duty (TAD) Travel. All Active Duty (AD), Active
Reserve (AR), US Navy (USN) and Navy Reserve (USNR) utilizing TEEP funding
will submit a T3 request and schedule travel in DTS. All Non-TEEP funded TAD
travel will schedule travel in DTS and no T3 TEEP request is required.

f. Upon creation of a TEEP request for travel and prior to submission for
approval, travel orders (DTS/MROWS) must be generated and submitted through
the appropriate level identified below:

(1) DTS. A DTS record must be created and a DTS document name must be
established.
(2) MROWS. A tracking number must be generated and routing complete to fund
approval.
(3) The Manning Government Travel Request (GTR) attached to the TEEP request
must contain: (a) Last name, first name and middle initial. (b) Electronic
Data Interchange Personal Identifier (EDIPI). (c) Date of birth. (d) Gender.
(e) Rank. (f) MROWS tracking number or DTS document name.
(4) Once the T3 request has been routed though the chain of command and is
approved by the MARFORRES or MSC T3 Manager, the DTS/MROWS orders must be
authenticated IAW paragraph 3i. in order to avoid a delay in the ticketing
process. It is the submitting unit's responsibility to ensure all T3 requests
and travel orders are accurate prior to approval.

(5) For group travel, the DMO sends the TOP request back to the MARFORRES/MSC
T3 Manager for "Reservations/Offers Approval", the T3 Manager must validate
that all passengers on the manifest have authenticated orders. Once the TOP
request is at DMO for "Ticketing", any passenger on the travel manifest
without authenticated orders will be dropped. **If orders are not approved by
15 days prior to departure date, reservations will be cancelled and the unit
will have to resubmit the request with MSC CoS waiver. Any new submission
within 15 days prior to departure date will also require MSC CoS waiver
justifying the late submission.**

g. **Reservations/Ticketing Process.**

(1) Unless limited by external constraints, reservations will be made within 5
to 10 days of receipt of the TOP request.
(2) Unless limited by external constraints, ticketing will occur within 10
days of date of travel. Ticket cost will be charged against the Centrally
Billed Account (CBA). To avoid credit card declines and reservation
cancellations, it is the traveler's and Agency Program Coordinator's (APC)
responsibility to ensure the IBA is activated at the time the DTS/MROWS orders
are submitted but NLT 10 days before date of travel.

h. **Manning and Transportation Changes.**

(1) All changes must be submitted, routed, and approved via T3 "Change
Request" in order for appropriate action to be taken by DMO. Changes are time
consuming and often result in excessive costs. Therefore, requests for date,
time, and location changes must be approved by the MSC and must be for
official reason and not for convenience, i.e. early termination of TAD.
(2) Cancellation of requests require annotation within the TOP Change Request
and remarks only require the verbiage "Cancel this request" and do not require
justification or description.
(3) Manning drops require annotation within the TOP Change Request and remarks
require the verbiage "Drop Said Name Marine (SNM)" and do not require
justification or further description.
(4) New personnel will not be added to an existing TOP request. Units wishing
to add new personnel to the manifest will need to submit a new T3 request. To
align the new traveler with a corresponding group, annotate the T3 request
identification number (ID) and Travel Order Number (TON) within the TOP
remarks. Adjudication is required in accordance with the Adjudication Process
in this order.

i. **TOP Submission Timelines for Non-SAAM flights ("D" equals departure
date).** (p. 5-6)

(1) D-65: Group Charter T3 request received by final approver MARFORRES/MSC T3
Manager.
(2) D-60: Group Charter TOP request received by DMO.
(3) D-50: OCONUS Commercial air T3 request (Individual/Group) received by
final approver MARFORRES/MSC T3 Manager.
(4) D-45: OCONUS Commercial air TOP request received by DMO with approved or
authenticated orders.
(5) D-35: CONUS commercial air/bus TOP request (individual and group) received
by MARFORRES/MSC T3 Manager.
(6) D-30: CONUS commercial air/bus TOP request (individual and group) received
by DMO with authenticated orders.
(7) D-10: Travelers Government Travel Charge Card (GTCC) IBA activated. A copy
of authenticated orders must be attached to the TOP request or DMO will cancel
request. A copy of the authenticated orders can be attached to the TOP request
any time prior to ticketing.
(8) D-10: DMO ticketing begins.
(9) D-5: DMO ticketing complete.

j. **Travel Adjudication Process.** (p. 5-6) Requires email endorsement to be
attached to respective TEEP request summary page. Endorsement needs to include
operational necessity, acceptance of all associated costs and acceptance of
risk caused by lack of carrier availability.

(1) MSC G-3/5 endorsement required for all respective timelines exceeded in
above paragraph and up to D-16 of execution.
(2) MSC Chief of Staff (CoS) endorsement required for all respective timelines
exceeded that are within 15 days of execution.

## 4. Strategic Mobility Support to TOP and TOT Requirements (pp. 5-6 to 5-7)

a. The SMO coordinates all strategic airlift (STRATLIFT) through USTRANSCOM.
STRATLIFT is utilized to move personnel and equipment to OCONUS locations in
support of approved exercises and operations. The SMO receives all TOP and TOT
requirements from units and MSCs through the Sea Service Deployment Module
(SSDM). It is imperative all data within SSDM is accurate and reflects proper
dimensions, weights, passenger numbers, and locations.

b. TOP: OCONUS moves with 100 passengers or more are requested by the SMO
utilizing the Special Assignment Airlift Mission (SAAM) Request System (SRS),
not T3. Units must still utilize T3 to submit their TOP request for movement
from HTC to the POE.

c. TOT: OCONUS moves of organic equipment and supplies (HMMWVs, MTVRs,
trailers, QUADCONs, etc.) are requested utilizing SRS if directed to move via
air, or an Export Traffic Release Request if directed to move via sea. Data
from SSDM is transferred into SRS for sourcing of aircraft or the Integrated
Booking System for sourcing surface vessels. USTRANSCOM will allocate the
appropriate lift based on the requirement submitted. Units must still utilize
T3 to submit their TOT request for movement from the HTC to the POE.

## 5. Exercise Support (EXSPT) (p. 5-7)

a. Funding Subhead 1A1A — Operations, Maintenance, Marine Corps Reserve
(O&MMCR). b. Funds material and services requirements for MARFORRES approved
exercises or unit ATs. c. Classes III and IV (BOMs), Class IX block
requirements, contract services (port-a-johns, dumpsters, fuel, telephone,
etc.), contract maintenance support, leased vehicles, GME vehicles, hazardous
material disposal, and Class VIII medical supplies in the form of AMALS/ADALS
replenishment. d. Exercise Support funds are intended for direct support of
approved TEEP exercises. e. **Exercise Support is not to be used for a unit's
drill weekend, unless it is in direct support of an approved TEEP exercise.**
f. Unit must request funds via TEEP request in T3.

## 6. Post Exercise Maintenance (PEM) (p. 5-7)

a. Funding Subhead 1A1A — Operations, Maintenance, Marine Corps Reserve
(O&MMCR). b. Funds cost to repair equipment use in support of a TEEP event.
Must perform pre and post LTI's and register in GCSS-MC (Pre LTI prior to
execution in GCSS-MC and Post LTI uploaded within 30 days upon completion of
exercise). c. Unit must request funds via TEEP request in T3. d. PEM events
can only be created by the MARFORRES TEEP Manager.
