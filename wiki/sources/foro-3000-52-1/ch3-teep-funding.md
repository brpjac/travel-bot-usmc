# ForO 3000-52.1, Chapter 3 — TEEP Exercise Funding (verbatim)

## 1. Introduction (p. 3-1)

a. Due to the multiple ways Reserve participation in Chairman of the Joint
Chiefs of Staff (CJCS), Service and MARFORRES training events can be
resourced, funding TEEP events can be both complex and confusing.
Additionally, the availability of funds can be a primary determinant of the
level of MARFORRES participation in training events. At the beginning of each
fiscal year the MARFORRES T3 Manager provides the MSCs with their funding
controls based on requirements developed at the Force Synchronization
Conference (FSC) and MARFORRES TEEP Conference. The controls are given by MSC,
by quarter and by funding category. Commanders will prioritize their training
events in line with priorities set by the chain of command and are required to
operate within the limits of their respective TEEP controls.

b. With few exceptions, there are two major appropriations for funding Reserve
training at MARFORRES. These are Reserve Personnel Marine Corps (RPMC) or
Budget Execution Authority (BEA) 1108; and Operations and Maintenance Marine
Corps Reserve (OMMCR) or BEA 1107. While reference (a) discusses in detail the
different categories of funding, the following describes the funding streams
used in creating/scheduling TEEP funded events and how they are applied, to
include the Budget Execution Sub Authorities (BESA) and the appropriate
Program Identifiers (PIDs) to be ran in the Marine Resource Order Writing
Service (MROWS).

## 2. Reserve Personnel Marine Corps (RPMC or 1108 funding) (pp. 3-1 to 3-2)

RPMC includes funds for TEEP Active Duty Operational Support (ADOS) and
Reserve Travel.

a. **TEEP ADOS (1108 sub-category designated 2732 funds).** (1) Funds pay and
allowances, per diem and travel for reservists. (2) RPMC funded ADOS can be
used to place Reserve planners on orders for TEEP events or to fund costs of
reservists on active duty beyond the normal 15 day annual training (AT)
period. (3) The program identifier (PID) code is PID 3 in MROWS. (4) TEEP ADOS
orders require DTS for travel authorization and transportation, but a T3 TEEP
request is still required to access funding.

b. **Reserve Travel (1108 sub-category designated 2731 funds).** Funds AT
Travel, contract AT billeting, Inactive Duty Training (IDT) travel and per
diem for offsite drills and chartered buses for drill weekends. The following
applies: (1) AT Travel funds travel and per diem for individual reservist on
AT. PID 1 code in MROWS. (2) Group Commercial Travel funds travel and per diem
for reservists on AT traveling in groups of 2 to 99 passengers. Group
Commercial TOP requests are submitted through T3 database. PID 1 code in
MROWS. (3) Commercial Charter also funds travel for reservists on AT traveling
in groups of 100 or more. (4) AT billeting funds contracted billeting while on
AT. (5) Offsite Inactive Duty Training (IDT) travel funds travel and per diem
for reservists while on an offsite drill. The offsite drill must be in direct
support of an approved TEEP funded event. PID 2 code in MROWS. (6) Chartered
buses provides funding for bus movements on drill weekend. TOP request must be
submitted through T3 database.

## 3. Operations and Maintenance Marine Corps Reserve (O&MMCR or 1107 funding) (pp. 3-2 to 3-3)

a. **Exercise Support Funds.** (1) Exercise Support Funds pay for consumable
materials commonly known as "Bill of Material" in addition to services
supporting the units in executing TEEP funded events. Exercise Support Funds
are authorized for COMMARFORRES approved exercises or unit ATs. (2) Classes of
Supply funded through Exercise Support include: Class III, Petroleum, Oil &
Lubricant (POL); Class IV Construction Material; Class IX repair part blocks
and secondary repairables; contract services such as port-a-johns, dumpsters,
fuel, telephone, etc.; contract maintenance support, leased vehicles, garrison
mobile equipment (GME) vehicles, and hazardous material disposal; and Class
VIII medical supplies in the form of Authorized Medical Allowance
List/Authorized Dental Allowance Lists (AMAL/ADAL) replenishment. (3) Exercise
Support Funds are intended for direct support of approved TEEP funded
exercises. (4) Exercise Support Funds are authorized to be used for a unit's
drill weekend, unless the IDT is in direct support of an approved TEEP funded
exercise. *(Transcriber note: sentence as printed; the intended meaning is
likely "are NOT authorized... unless" — flagged for verification.)*

b. **Temporary Additional Duty (TAD)** (p. 3-3). TAD funding covers travel and
per diem for Active Component (AC) and Active Reserve (AR) Marines traveling
on missions in support of Reserve participation in authorized TEEP events in
T3. TAD funding also covers travel and per diem for active duty and reserve
Navy personnel assigned to Marine reserve units.

c. **Transportation of Things (TOT)** (p. 3-3). TOT funding pays for
transporting equipment and/or supplies. TOT requests must be approved in the
T3 database prior to equipment shipment for all TEEP events.

d. **Post Exercise Maintenance (PEM)** (p. 3-3). PEM funding provides money to
cover the cost to repair equipment used in support of a TEEP event. Units are
required to perform both pre- and post-exercise Limited Technical Inspections
(LTIs) and register the results in GCSS-MC. The pre-LTI will be loaded into
GCSS-MC prior to execution while the post-LTI is to be uploaded into GCSS-MC
within 30 days of completing the exercise. Coordination between exercise
planners and the MARFORRES T3 Manager is required as PEM events are created at
the MARFORRES level by the T3 Manager.

## 4. Joint Exercise Program (JEP) Funding (0100 funding) (pp. 3-3 to 3-4)

a. **Commercial Ticketing Program (CTP).** (1) CTP is only used in support of
CJCS exercises as defined in reference (b). (2) CTP funds commercial airline
tickets in support of a JEP sponsored exercise. Note: CTP only funds the
airline ticket; it does not fund per diem. (3) To receive CTP, the unit must
be listed in the exercise Time Phased Force Deployment Data (TPFDD) and
authorized by the supported MARFOR. (4) For SMCR individual or group
commercial travel use PID CTP code in MROWS in combination with PID 1 pay &
allowances and per diem or PID CTA code if used in combination with PID 3 ADOS
pay & allowances and per diem.

b. **Service Incremental Funding (SIF).** (1) Only used in support of CJCS
exercises. (2) Covers cost of travel and per diem while attending CJCS
sponsored planning conferences. Does not cover pay and allowances for reserve
personnel. (3) AC and AR personnel, to include active duty Navy personnel
assigned to MARFORRES, will be funded through Defense Travel System (DTS)
cross organization code established by the supported MARFOR. Units must submit
a roster of travelers via MARFORRES G-3/5 for approval by the supported
MARFOR. (4) Reserve personnel will be funded through MROWS using PID SIF for
travel and per diem, while PID 3 must be used for pay and allowances (P&A).
Units must submit a roster of travelers via MARFORRES G-3/5 for approval by
the supported MARFOR. (5) SIF covers the cost of Bill of Materials (BOM) for
participating units in CJCS sponsored exercises. Funds are provided directly
to the unit through the MARFORRES G-3/5 and the Comptroller. Units are not
authorized to use TEEP Exercise Support Funds for BOMs to be paid for with
SIF. If the unit programs Exercise Support Funds when SIF is the appropriate
funding stream, the unit cannot be reimbursed and the money will be
decremented from their MSC Exercise Support fund allocation. (6) SIF also
covers the cost of Exercise Support requirements paid to the host nation by
the supported MARFOR. The use of TEEP Exercise Support Funds for CJCS
exercises is not authorized.

## 5. Innovative Readiness Training (IRT) (p. 3-4)

a. **Operations & Maintenance (O&M).** (1) O&M for IRT funds TAD costs for
active component and active reserve personnel participating in IRT sponsored
projects. (2) O&M for IRT funds TOT and Exercise Support costs for authorized
IRT projects. (3) O&M for IRT funds the travel and per diem for reserve
personnel on long-term IRT ADOS. (4) Requests for IRT O&M funds are made
through the T3 database.

b. **ADOS.** (1) IRT funding will provide funds for Selected Reserve IRT
Project Managers to be assigned to active duty via long-term ADOS (30 to 365
days) to provide continuity in support for the projects. (2) IRT funding will
provide funds for conference attendance and short term ADOS for reserve
personnel. (3) The code to use in MROWs is PID IRT.

## 6. Active Component ADOS Funding for Reserve Support (pp. 3-4 to 3-5)

a. Provided by the supported MARFOR and pays for pay and allowances for
reserves on active duty in direct support of the Active Component. Funding is
sourced from active component Military Personnel Marine Corps (MPMC) ADOS
allocated to the MARFORs for reserve augmentation.

b. Additionally, the travel or per diem associated with ADOS requires O&MMC
from Active Component to pay for travel and per diem via DTS.

## 7. Extended Annual Training (AT) / Split AT / Additional AT (p. 3-5)

a. The standard AT period is 14 days plus one day for travel for a total of
15 days as defined by reference (c). With approval of COMMARFORRES G-3/5, the
standard AT can be extended up to 29 days inclusive of travel. The total
number of AT days shall not exceed 29 days at any time.

b. A "Split" AT divides a standard AT into two separate periods, the total
number of days cannot exceed 15 days. MSC G3s can approve split ATs without
MARFORRES approval. IAW reference (c), an AT may only be split once.

c. An "Additional" AT is a second AT of 15 days; the total number of days for
both the first and second ATs cannot exceed 30 days. Extended AT days cannot
be used for an additional AT.

d. Priorities for Extended ATs and Additional ATs: (1) Pre-mobilization
training in support of war time operations and contingencies. (2) CJCS
sponsored exercises. (3) Non-CJCS OCONUS exercises when approved by
COMMARFORRES. (4) Other events as determined by COMMARFORRES.

e. At the start of each FY, MARFORRES G-3/5 will publish a list of
COMMARFORRES approved extended ATs, to include duration.

f. Requests for extended ATs and Additional ATs must be approved by MSC
Commanders and submitted to COMMARFORRES via Automated Messaging Handling
System (AMHS). Requests must include a justification, number of Marines, total
number of AT days for each Marine and the impact on the unit if the extension
is not granted.

g. Extended AT and Additional AT orders submitted in MROWS must be approved by
MARFORRES G-3/5 with the approval of the exercise by COMMARFORRES. Orders must
contain the TEEP event number and the proper Special Interest Code (SIC)
identifying the exercise.
