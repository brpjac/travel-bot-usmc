# ForO 3000-52.1, Chapter 2 — Development of TEEP and Non-TEEP Events (verbatim)

## 1. Introduction (p. 2-1)

The T3 database is the MARFORRES Program of Record designed to identify
required unit, personnel, equipment and resources for the efficient and
effective execution of training exercises and exercise deployments. Inputs to
the T3 database include both TEEP and Non-TEEP funded events allowing the
Command to track the use of resources and define requirements for approved
unit participation in a training event. The T3 database provides visibility of
all events in a given fiscal year.

## 2. Background (p. 2-1)

The process of placing exercises and TEEP funded events begins with MARFORRES
participation in the Global Force Management (GFM) process via the Service
(Plans, Policies & Operations (PP&O)) directed and Marine Forces Command
(MARFORCOM) hosted quarterly Force Synchronization Conferences (FSC). All
geographical MARFORs and all the MEFs are represented and present their force
requirements. MARFORRES sends planners from the G-3/5 Regional Operations and
Plans Division as well as planners from the MSC respective G-3/5s. The Force
Synchronization Conference is where Joint and Service exercise requirements
are identified and sourced in addition to Theater Security Cooperation (TSC)
events, Active Component support requirements to also include contingencies
and community relations events, e.g., New York City Fleet Week. These events
are captured in the USMC Force Management Tool (formerly known as Playbook)
and the MARFORRES/MSC planners will identify personnel, equipment and funding
requirements in the T3 Database. Additional requirements for the T3 database
will also include Non-TEEP funded events which are usually coordinated
internally at MSC/MARFORRES TEEP Conferences.

## 3. Prioritization for TEEP Funded Events (p. 2-1)

Prioritization of TEEP funded events will align with MARFORRES sourcing
priorities. The primary sourcing priority for MARFORRES planners at the Force
Synchronization Conference is augmentation/reinforcement of active component
units engaged in combat operations followed by other GFM requirements also
requiring activation of Reserve units and detachments. While activations are
not funded through the TEEP, the TEEP is the source of funds for
pre-activation pre-deployment training events. Priority GFM activations will
be followed by exercises. Exercise priorities are as follows: (1) GFM Chairman
of Joint Chiefs of Staff (CJCS) Exercises, (2) ITX-Reserve, (3) other Service
Level Training Exercises (SLTEs) and (4) bilateral exercises. Considerations
for planners when sourcing exercises: a. Provides pre-deployment training for
activation units. b. Provides training to unit METs. c. Provides OPTEMPO
relief to the Active Component. d. Availability of Joint or Combatant
Commander Theater Security Cooperation (TSC) funds. e. Provides integrated
training with Active Component. f. Exercise can be funded by MSC or MARFORRES
TEEP allocation/budget.

## 4. Authorized TEEP Events (p. 2-2)

Below types of events are authorized for inclusion in the MARFORRES TEEP:

a. MARFORRES sponsored events: COMMARFORES designated mandatory events for
assigned MSC units, i.e. Integrated Training Exercise (ITX).
b. Joint Exercise Program events as listed in the Joint Training Information
Management System (JTIMS).
c. Events, other than JEP exercises that involve joint participation of
Reserve units and other Service units.
d. Events, other than JEP exercises, that provides direct OPTEMPO relief to an
active duty unit.
e. Integrated Training: Events involving participation of Reserve and Active
Component units.
f. Innovative Readiness Training (IRT): Events that derive training value to a
unit while utilizing its organic capability to provide a community benefit.
g. Deployment for Training OCONUS events.
h. MSC directed training exercises.
i. Unit training as it relates to exercises. Individual skills, licensing
courses and training symposiums do not qualify as TEEP events.
j. Conferences supporting any of the above events. The number of conferences
for any single event is dictated by MARFORRES or the respective MSC and is
dependent on the availability of funds.

## 5. TEEP Budgeting Process (pp. 2-2 to 2-3)

a. MARFORRES will host a TEEP conference to identify requirements for TEEP
funding in the following fiscal year. The requirements will be submitted to
the MARFORRES Comptroller who will publish fiscal guidance in the July/August
time period based on requirements and funds available. The guidance will
include funding estimates for the TEEP. These figures are only estimates to
allow for planning, however the estimates may be adjusted by the Comptroller
based on availability of funding.

b. The MARFORRES G-3/5 T3 Manager uses the guidance in the planning estimates
as the basics for the submission of the upcoming FY TEEP Spend Plan into the
Memorandum Fiscal Services (MFS) database.

c. The TEEP Spend Plan is developed by funding category, quarters, and MSC.
The MSCs are then provided their allotment of funds for planning. The MSCs
initially prioritize their TEEP funding based upon COMMARFORRES mission
priorities, after which the MSCs prioritize their remaining TEEP events for
funding.

d. Upon the sourcing of a unit as the TEEP event participant, that unit will
estimate the funds required by fiscal category (ADOS, TAD, TOT, TOP, etc.) and
enter a TEEP request into the T3 database. Once the request has been
submitted, it will process via the TEEP approval chain within the T3 and if
approved, funds will be available for execution.

## 6. Non-TEEP Funded Events (p. 2-3)

The T3 database is also used to track many events that do not qualify as TEEP
funded events. These Non-TEEP funded events are funded separately, have
separate budget lines and are not included in the TEEP planning estimates or
TEEP Spend Plan.

## 7. Authorized Non-TEEP Funded Events (p. 2-3)

Types of events authorized for inclusion in the T3 database to account for
funded travel of personnel, equipment and supplies but funded separately:

a. Schools and specialized training, i.e., combatant commander required in
preparation for an activation.
b. Professional Military Education and other formal schools.
c. Redistribution of equipment between MARFORRES units.
d. Shipments of supplies using Small Package Program (FedEx, UPS, etc.)
e. Administrative travel for personnel, i.e., TAD not associated with TEEP
events.
f. MARFORRES Band movements.

## 8. Non-TEEP Budgeting Process (pp. 2-3 to 2-4)

a. The MARFORRES Comptroller will publish fiscal guidance in the July/August
time period based on requirements and funds available. The guidance will
contain the planning estimates for the upcoming fiscal year to include
estimates for Non-TEEP funded events. Planning estimates are given to program
managers of Non-TEEP funds, e.g., Formal Schools Manager and the MSCs to plan
for the upcoming fiscal year. These figures are only estimates to allow for
planning; however, the estimates may be adjusted by the Comptroller based on
availability of funding.

b. The MSCs, their subordinate units, and program managers register these
funding requirements in MFS database.
