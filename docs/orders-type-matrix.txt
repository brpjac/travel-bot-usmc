===================================================================
HOW TO DETERMINE YOUR ORDERS TYPE AND SUBMISSION REQUIREMENTS
===================================================================

This matrix tells you what type of orders to request and what you need to submit based on your service type, destination, and trip purpose.

---

SERVICE TYPE: SMCR (Selected Marine Corps Reserve)

  DESTINATION: Home Training Center (HTC)
    ORDERS TYPE: IDT (Inactive Duty Training)
    REQUIREMENTS:
      - Submit $750 travel voucher NLT 15 days prior to trip in DTS-R
      - Fill out Billeting Request Form with S-4 if lodging is required
      - Notify MIU_Operations@usmc.mil NLT 1 week prior to travel

  DESTINATION: More than 50 miles from HTC, CONUS (Continental United States)

    ORDERS TYPE: Offsite IDT
    REQUIREMENTS:
      - Submit Orders Request Form in Correspondence Tracker (45 days prior to travel)
      - Must be current on: Medical, GTCC, Primary Residence

    ORDERS TYPE: AT (Annual Training) — use for trips longer than 7 days
    REQUIREMENTS:
      - Submit Orders Request Form in Correspondence Tracker (45 days prior to travel)
      - Must be current on: Medical, GTCC, Primary Residence

    ORDERS TYPE: ADOS (Active Duty Operational Support) — use for trips shorter than 7 days
    REQUIREMENTS:
      - Submit Orders Request Form in Correspondence Tracker (NLT 2 weeks prior to travel)
      - Must be current on: Medical, GTCC, Primary Residence

  DESTINATION: OCONUS (Outside Continental United States)

    ORDERS TYPE: Offsite IDT
    REQUIREMENTS:
      - Submit Orders Request Form in Correspondence Tracker (60 days prior to travel)
      - Must be current on: Medical, GTCC, Primary Residence, APACS

    ORDERS TYPE: AT (Annual Training) — use for trips longer than 7 days
    REQUIREMENTS:
      - Submit Orders Request Form in Correspondence Tracker (60 days prior to travel)
      - Must be current on: Medical, GTCC, Primary Residence, APACS

    ORDERS TYPE: ADOS (Active Duty Operational Support) — use for trips shorter than 7 days
    REQUIREMENTS:
      - Submit Orders Request Form in Correspondence Tracker (NLT 3 weeks prior to travel)
      - Must be current on: Medical, GTCC, Primary Residence, APACS

---

SERVICE TYPE: Active Reserve
  DESTINATION: HTC or Anywhere
    ORDERS: Created and managed through DTS (Defense Travel System)
    NOTE: DTS is the travel management system, not an orders type. Active Reserve Marines use standard DTS process for all travel.

---

SERVICE TYPE: Active Duty
  DESTINATION: HTC or Anywhere
    ORDERS: Created and managed through DTS (Defense Travel System)
    NOTE: DTS is the travel management system, not an orders type. Active Duty Marines use standard DTS process for all travel.

===================================================================
ENTITLEMENTS BY ORDERS TYPE
===================================================================

IDT (Inactive Duty Training):
  - NO per diem, NO rental car, NO commercial travel reimbursement
  - Lodging: Only available at HTC (on-base billeting if available)
  - Mileage: $750 travel voucher via DTS-R
  - Exception: CO/Authorizing Official can authorize lodging with a written travel order
    if overnight stay is mission-required and duty period exceeds 12 hours

Offsite IDT:
  - Per diem: YES (meals and incidentals)
  - Lodging: YES (if authorized on orders)
  - Rental car: May be authorized if mission-required and approved by AO
  - Mileage: YES (POV reimbursement at standard rate)

AT (Annual Training):
  - Per diem: YES
  - Lodging: YES
  - Rental car: May be authorized if advantageous to the Government
  - Commercial travel: YES (air/rail if distance warrants)
  - Full TDY entitlements apply

ADOS (Active Duty Operational Support):
  - Per diem: YES
  - Lodging: YES
  - Rental car: YES (if authorized — most common orders type for rental car entitlement)
  - Commercial travel: YES
  - Full TDY entitlements apply
  - Note: ADOS is often the best option when a Marine needs travel entitlements
    (lodging, rental car, per diem) for a short-duration trip

===================================================================
ITEMS OF NOTE
===================================================================

1. IDT Lodging: Conduct drills (IDTs) for pay on Federal Installations. IDT lodging is only available at HTC, unless submitting for Offsite IDT orders.

2. Courtesy Drills: Work with DET leadership to submit in RTAMMS. Courtesy unit musters.

3. Appropriate Duty: NAVMC 799 submitted to DET leadership via MIU Drill Submission Page and uploaded into Drill Manager.

4. Under 50 miles from HTC: Default orders type is IDT (no travel entitlements). If the Marine needs lodging, rental car, or per diem, options are: (a) request CO exception/waiver on IDT for lodging if mission-required, or (b) request ADOS orders which provide full TDY entitlements including rental car.

===================================================================
KEY DEFINITIONS
===================================================================

- HTC: Home Training Center — the location where a Marine is assigned to drill
- CONUS: Continental United States (the 48 contiguous states plus DC)
- OCONUS: Outside Continental United States (Alaska, Hawaii, territories, overseas)
- IDT: Inactive Duty Training — standard drill periods
- AT: Annual Training — typically 12-17 day training periods
- ADOS: Active Duty Operational Support — short-term active duty support
- SMCR: Selected Marine Corps Reserve
- DTS: Defense Travel System
- DTS-R: Defense Travel System for Reserves
- GTCC: Government Travel Charge Card
- APACS: Aircraft and Personnel Automated Clearance System (required for OCONUS travel)
- RTAMMS: Reserve Training and Administration Management System
- NLT: No Later Than
